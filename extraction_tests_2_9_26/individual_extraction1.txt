The tracheo-bronchial tree was then examined to at least the first subsegmental level and mucinous secretions were mobilized with gentle suction. The scope was advance to the 11L nodal station and a 1.8mm RX needle Knife XL was introduced into the working channel. A grounding pad was placed and the FiO2 was lowered. When EtO2 was confirmed to be below 40 a small tract was then created utilizing electrocautery. The therapeutic scope was then carefully removed from the airway. An EBUS scope was then reintroduced through the LMA and advanced to the 11L nodal site. Under white light a 1.7mm cryo-probe was advanced through the tract into the 11L node under direct visualization. EBUS was utilized to confirm intranodal placement of the cryo probe. Once placement was confirmed, intranodal cryobiopsies were obtained x3. The EBUS scope was then withdrawn from the airway. 

Finally, inspection bronchoscopy was performed. A scant amount of coagulated blood was removed with gentle suction. Following inspection the sscope was genetly removed from the airway. 

The procedure was accomplished without difficulty. The patient tolerated the procedure well without immediate complications.
